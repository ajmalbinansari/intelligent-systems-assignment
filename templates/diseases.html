Plant Disease Information

This section provides information about common plant diseases that our system can detect. Each entry includes symptoms, causes, and management strategies to help you maintain healthy plants and crops.

Tomato Late Blight

A devastating disease caused by the pathogen Phytophthora infestans.

Symptoms

Dark brown spots on leaves with pale green borders. White fungal growth may appear on undersides in humid conditions. Stems and fruits can also be infected.

Management
  • Remove infected plants promptly
  • Improve air circulation
  • Avoid overhead watering
  • Apply copper-based fungicides preventatively
  • Plant resistant varieties when possible

Tomato Early Blight

A common fungal disease caused by Alternaria solani.

Symptoms

Dark brown to black spots with concentric rings on lower/older leaves first. Yellowing around lesions and premature leaf drop.

Management
  • Practice crop rotation
  • Maintain adequate plant spacing
  • Mulch around plants
  • Remove infected leaves
  • Apply appropriate fungicides if necessary

Apple Scab

A fungal disease caused by Venturia inaequalis that affects apple trees.

Symptoms

Olive-green or brown spots on leaves and fruit. Severe infections cause leaf drop and fruit deformation.

Management
  • Proper sanitation (remove fallen leaves)
  • Plant resistant varieties
  • Apply preventative fungicidal sprays
  • Prune to improve air circulation

Corn Common Rust

A fungal disease caused by Puccinia sorghi.

Symptoms

Small, circular to elongated brown pustules on both leaf surfaces. Severe cases can cause leaf death.

Management
  • Plant resistant hybrids
  • Apply fungicides if detected early
  • Practice good field sanitation
  • Ensure proper plant spacing for air circulation

Potato Late Blight

Caused by the same pathogen as Tomato Late Blight (Phytophthora infestans).

Symptoms

Water-soaked spots on leaves that quickly enlarge and turn brown with a white fuzzy growth. Tubers develop dark patches.

Management
  • Use certified disease-free seed potatoes
  • Maintain good air circulation
  • Apply preventative fungicides
  • Harvest during dry weather
  • Remove volunteer plants

Grape Black Rot

A fungal disease caused by Guignardia bidwellii.

Symptoms

Small, reddish-brown spots on leaves that later develop a black border. Fruit develops black rot with shriveling.

Management
  • Prune to increase air circulation
  • Remove mummified fruits
  • Apply appropriate fungicides
  • Practice good vineyard sanitation
